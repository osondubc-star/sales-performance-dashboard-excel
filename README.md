# sales-performance-dashboard-excel
Interactive Excel dashboard analyzing sales performance across products, sales representatives, cities, and monthly trends with business insights and recommendations.

#  Project Overview
This project was assigned as part of TS Academy's Excel Course, under the instruction of Mr. Ezekiel Aleke. It explores the sales performance of a company over 5 months (January – May 2025) across 4 cities, 10 products, 3 product categories and 6 sales representatives.

**Tool Used:**  Microsoft Excel 

**Skills Applied:**  KPI calculation, Pivot Tables, Pivot Charts, Timeline Slicer, Data Slicers, Dashboard Design

#  Dashboard Features
1.  4 KPI Cards — Revenue, COGS, Profit, Total Transactions
2.  Profit Margin indicator — 20%
3.  Overview section with real-time insight description
4.   4 Interactive Charts
5.  Timeline Slicer (January – May 2025)
6.  2 Slicers — Region & Category
7.  Icon navigation panel
8.  Insights & Recommendations sheet
9.  KPI Cards Close-up

## KEY INSIGHTS & FINDINGS

1. **Top Profiting Products**
Laptop A13 led all products with approximately $105.34M in profit, followed by Sofa Classic at $69.25M. The significant gap between the top product and others signals an over-dependence on Laptop A13. If this product goes out of stock or the manufacturer discontinues it, the business risks a substantial drop in profitability overnight. It is recommended that the company actively promote and strengthen other product lines to reduce this concentration risk.

2. **Revenue by Sales Representative**
Peter generated the highest revenue at $434.81M, outperforming his closest colleague by a noticeable margin. This raises an important question — what strategies is Peter applying that others are not? Management should analyze his approach and use it to coach the broader sales team. Introducing performance-based bonuses and incentive programs could also motivate other representatives to increase their output.

3. **Monthly Transactions & Revenue Trends**
April recorded the highest number of transactions at 536
March generated the highest revenue at $353.75M
This discrepancy shows that more transactions do not always mean more revenue — the value per sale matters just as much as volume
May recorded only 18 transactions, which is not a true performance drop but most likely a result of incomplete data collection for that month. Analysts should always flag sudden drops like this before drawing conclusions.

4. **COGS by City**
Lagos recorded the highest COGS at $493.81M, followed by Kano ($486.79M), Port Harcourt ($458.78M) and Abuja ($423.31M). The narrow gap between all four cities indicates a relatively balanced cost distribution across locations — a positive sign of operational consistency across regions.

 
## Recommendations
— Diversify product focus, reduce over-reliance on Laptop A13 by investing in marketing and promotions for other products
— Replicate top performer strategies, study Peter's sales approach and document it for team-wide coaching
— Introduce performance incentives — reward-based programs can drive motivation across the sales team
— Investigate data completeness — ensure May data is fully captured before drawing month-on-month conclusions
— Monitor regional balance, the even COGS distribution across cities is healthy; maintain this consistency going forward
